Master of Applied Positive Psychology

Positive psychology has developed a scientific understanding of how humans flourish and how communities and institutions can be strengthened. It provides a framework for effective practice in a wide range of professional contexts.

The Master of Applied Positive Psychology is the first course of its kind in Australia. It will equip you to apply positive psychology principles in your professional and personal life, with a special focus on creating and evaluating positive and meaningful change and promoting optimal leadership within organisations.

Learning and assessment will take place through a range of tasks, including debates, case studies, role plays, videos, journal entries and research activities. In addition, you will be encouraged to apply positive psychology principles to your own life and to critically reflect on these experiences.

This course promotes an evidence-based, multidisciplinary perspective to working in positive psychology, and will instil a strong sense of professional integrity and civic engagement in graduates.

Application Process

All applicants are required to complete each of the following steps:

  1. Complete a online application for local students or international students
  2. Submit a copy of previous academic transcripts
  3. Submit a copy of your CV
  4. Complete and submit the two-page Statement of Intent(31kb) document
Applications for Semester 1, 2014 close 13 December. 

Please attach relevant documents to the online application. Alternatively, these can be mailed to the Melbourne Graduate School of Education student centre at 234 Queensberry St, University of Melbourne, VIC, 3010.

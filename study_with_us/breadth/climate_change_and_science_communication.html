Climate change and science communication

Introduction to climate change

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: 2 one hour lectures and 1 one hour tutorial per week

Level: 1

Assessment details:
  • research essay (2000 words), 30%, due during semester;
  • weekly practical exercises due in tutorials, 40%;
  • a 2-hour written examination in the examination period, 30%

This subject will provide a comprehensive and up-to-date introduction to all aspects of the climate change debate.

Concepts covered include the scientific basis of the greenhouse effect, the history of Earth’s climate, energy options, economics and public policy and the effect of climate change on food, water and health.

You will have the opportunity to interact with Australian scientific and political leaders with expertise and interests in climate change, and consider information within your own local context.

Further information: Maurizio Toscano: m.toscano@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook.

Communicating science and technology

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: 2 hour seminar and 1 hour tutorial per week

Level: 2

Assessment details:
  • written assignment (1000 words) and peer review of other students’ written assignments, 30%, due week 8;
  • online science blog (1,500 word minimum), 30%, due throughout semester;
  • group project (approx. 60 hours), 40%, due end of semester

Consider the important role of science and technology and explore why scientists must learn to articulate their ideas to a variety of audiences in an effective and engaging manner.

Learn how to create engaging science and technology communication, by tailoring communication to audience needs, using the right type of media and creating good science writing. Other topics include talking about science on the radio, effective public speaking, writing press releases and science feature articles, science performance and how science is reported in the media.

You will be given multiple opportunities to receive feedback and improve your own written and oral communication skills.

Further information: Maurizio Toscano: m.toscano@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook.

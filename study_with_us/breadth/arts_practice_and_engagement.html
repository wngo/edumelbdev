Arts practice and engagement

Drawing, painting and sensory knowing

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: One 3 hour workshop per week

Level: 1

Assessment details:
  • visual arts folio (1,600 words equivalent), 40%, due week 8;
  • art exhibition review (800 words), 20%, due week 11;
  • individual project presentation and reflective workbook (1,600 words), 40%, due week 13.

Explore a diverse range of painting and drawing subjects and methods, inspired by personal experiences and visits to museums and galleries. Develop your ‘sensory knowing’ and individual expression through art. You will respond to and learn about the world around you through practical studies in drawing and painting, exploring how both children and adults make and create through the visual arts.

Further information: Robert Brown r.brown@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook.

Spontaneous drama improv and learning communities

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: 3 hour workshop per week

Level: 1

Assessment details:
  • research project (1,600 words), 40%, due week 8;
  • group presentation / performance (30 minutes), 40%, due week 12;
  • reflective journal (800 words), 20%, due week 13

Learn about improvised drama and theatre from the perspective of performer and audience, covering forms like improv, performance art, flash mobs and street theatre.

Learn about improvised drama and theatre from the perspective of performer and audience, covering forms like improv, performance art, flash mobs and street theatre.

You will take part in weekly drama/theatre improvisation workshops, exploring a range of improvisational forms, their application in the community and how they can be used for educational purposes. Forms include: street theatre, flash mobbing, applied theatre and Boal’s forum theatre, image theatre and invisible theatre.

You will also conduct a research project looking at how improvisation is used in the wider community to entertain, educate and inform. The subject will culminate in an improvisational class presentation and you will document your involvement in its development.

Further information: Richard Sallis: sallis@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook.

School of rock and informal education

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: 1 hour lecture and 2 hour workshop per week

Level: 1

Assessment details:
  • Music Venue Site Visit Project 25%;
  • Group music performance 25%;
  • Essay (1,500 words) with reference to course readings, reflection on the process and exegesis) 50%

Explore the nature of pop, rock, blues and folk musicians' informal learning practices, by forming your own band and selecting, composing /arranging, recording and performing your chosen music.

Many popular musicians acquire some or all of their skills and knowledge informally, outside school or university, often with little or no help from trained instrumental teachers. To learn more about how they go about this process, students will conduct research (including site visits), discuss and analyse music videos, music related digital technologies and selected readings.

Further information: Andrew Swainston: swaa@unimelb.edu.au.

Printing, collage and social engagement

Availability: Semester 1

Contact hours: 3 hour workshop every week

Level: 2

Assessment details:
  • Written analysis of collaborative art project (800 words), 20% due mid-semester;
  • visual arts folio (1,600 words equivalent), 40%, due week 12;
  • negotiated project presentation and theoretical commentary (1,600 words equivalent), 40%, due week 13.

This is for students with varied prior experiences in visual arts practice.

Explore the artmaking processes of printing and collage, and their diverse purposes as forms of communication and social engagement.

You will work in artistic collaboration with a local school, and in a negotiated community project. You will explore a range of printmaking techniques including photographic screenprinting, stencil printing, lino printing and collographs, as well as collage-making techniques.

Further information: Marnee Watkins m.watkins@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook.

Devising drama in learning communities

Availability: Semester 1

Contact hours: 3 hour workshop every week

Level: 2

Assessment details:
  • Research project 30%, due Week 5;
  • presentation/performance, 50%, due week 12;
  • reflective artist’s workbook, 20%, due Week 13

Explore the principles of performance-making across a range of different cultures and theatrical traditions. You will examine how and why theatre is devised, and how performance-making affects and is affected by audiences. Along with a teaching artist, you will participate in the development of an ensemble performance for a designated audience and performance venue.

You will keep a reflective artist’s workbook and conduct a research task into the practices and principles of an ensemble theatre company.

Further information: Christine Sinclair: cesi@unimelb.edu.au / Richard Sallis: sallis@unimelb.edu.au.

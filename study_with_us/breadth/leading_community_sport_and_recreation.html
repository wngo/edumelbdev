Sports coaching: theory and practice

Availability: One week intensive (February), Semester 1, Semester 2

Contact hours: 36 hours

Level: 1

Assessment details:
  • online tests related to readings, 25%, bi-weekly;
  • coaching unit and rational, 50%, exam period;
  • presentation, 25%, mid semester

An introduction to the practical and theoretical aspects of sport and exercise coaching, developing skills for the sports arena and the wider world. You will be encouraged to develop leadership, organisational, communication and analytical skills as well as a sound understanding of the theoretical and practical techniques used in coaching and how to apply them.

You will lead and participate in physical activity sessions and reflect on your own and other people's coaching practices. You will also create and present a session plan and a six-week training program.

At the end of the course you will achieve the Australian Sports Commissions Beginning Coaching General Principles certificate.

Further information: Mel Nash: mjnash@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook

Sport and education in Australia

Availability: Semester 1, Semester 2

Contact hours: 1 hour lecture and 2 hour workshop per week

Level: 2

Assessment details:
  • online quizzes throughout the semester, 20%;
  • case study of a sport related situation or scenario (1500 words), 30%, due mid semester;
  • analysis of the representation of a current sporting event/issue in the media (2000 words), 50%, due at the end of semester

To better understand the role of sport and physical education in Australia, we need to understand its history and the issues that revolve around it.

We examine young people’s connection with sport and physical education by considering the tension between sport’s traditional role as a form of moral and social education, and its emergence as a commodity.

As we progress through the semester, you will learn more about what constitutes a sport, how we define sport and how we determine what is relevant to children in their sporting education. We will examine current sporting issues, controversies and debates emerging in the media. We will also analyse the many issues to do with race, socio-economic status, culture and gender in connection with sports in Australia and internationally.

Further information: Anna Krohn: akrohn@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook

Learning via Sport and Outdoor Education

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: 18 hours of workshops, plus fieldtrips

Level: 2

Assessment details:
  • 2 x 1000-word reports on the field trip experience, 30% in total (mid semester);
  • 1 x 2000 word report on experiential learning from fieldtrips and classes, 70% (end of semester)
  • 1 x 2000 word report on experiential learning from fieldtrips and classes, 70% (end of semester)

Learning does not just happen in classrooms or lecture theatres. You will explore the close connections between learning and experience – particularly though two events: a fun run and a bushwalk.

You will train for and participate in a fun run, with the help of Melbourne University Athletics Club coaches. You will also do a 3-day bushwalk with qualified guides at Wilsons Promontory National Park.

By participating in these events and exploring theory in class, you will have taken part in an investigation of how learning occurs as experience.

Applying coaching science

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: 36

Level: 3

Assessment details:
  • coaching plan (1000 words) due mid-semester, 25%;
  • coaching report (1000 words) due end of semester, 25%;
  • six written tasks (total 2000 words) due fortnightly

Please note: Sports coaching: theory and practice is a pre-requisite for this subject

This subject is for students with proven coaching experience and a connection to a sporting club or organisation

Learn how sports science theory can be applied to improve athletic performance, and develop a coaching program for your own sports setting. You will develop individualised training programs using concepts such as periodisation, sports psychology, physiology, training age, growth and development factors and biomechanics.

You must hold a coaching position with a sports club (or school) as you will be expected to implement and evaluate your program.

Further information: Mel Nash: mjnash@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook

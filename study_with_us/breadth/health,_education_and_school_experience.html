Health, education and school experience

Aaron Osborne, Bachelor of Biomedicine

School Experience as Breadth

“I did this subject because I wanted to explore what teaching is like. I’ve found the subject really fun; doing science in primary classrooms has been great.

One girl enjoyed the experiments we did in class so much that she went home and changed the test to make a new discovery – then she came back to school and we discussed what she had learned. It was really exciting to get such a positive response to my lesson from a ten-year-old.

I’ve found the subject quite challenging – I’m not used to the assignments because they’re different to what we do in Biomed. But the lecturer has been really helpful and it’s good to do something different. I’ve really enjoyed having two hours a week studying something completely different!”

Relating health and learning

Availability: Semester 2

Contact hours: 1.5 hour lecture and 1.5 hour workshop per week

Level: 2

Assessment details:
  • essay (1,500 words), 35%, due early semester;
  • online discussion forum exercises (500 words), 15%, due throughout semester;
  • proposal / report (2,000 words), 50%, due end of semester

Explore the relationship between education and health, with a particular focus on the young child and young adult. You will discover more about the way we learn and how health influences learning productivity.

Using your own experiences, plus local and international examples, you will examine how health and learning activities work together to improve an individual's life. You will discuss health and education in the context of real world examples to understand what works, and how we can improve projects and programs.

You will explore how to apply many of these ideas in real world settings, improving both your own ability to learn and your ability to help others learn.

Further information: Janet Clinton: jclinton@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook.

School Experience as Breadth

Availability: Semesters 1 & 2

Contact hours: approx. 1 day per week in school; university classes before placement (9 hours) and during placement (6 hours)

Level: 2

Assessment details:
  • reflective journal of 4 x 600 word observational reflections (2400 words), 60% due early – mid semester;
  • report (1600 words), 40% due mid - late semester. Students must also lead a discussion during one of the seminars (this is a hurdle task).

Volunteer as a Tertiary Student Assistant (TSA) in a Victorian Government school, and learn about contemporary approaches to teaching and learning.

You will make links between your undergraduate degree and the wider community and get a taste of working in a school, by assisting in a languages, mathematics, science or arts classroom. School-based experience is supplemented by university workshops and seminars, to give you further support and ideas.

This subject is for students with high level skills in mathematics, science, arts or languages other than English (LOTE).

Further information: Rannah Hetherington: rannahms@unimelb.edu.au or see the Handbook

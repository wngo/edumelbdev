How to navigate the RHD application process

Below are some of the most common issues that can delay our processing of RHD applications. Please take the time to read through these as you complete the RHD application form.

Section A – Personal details

If you are an existing University of Melbourne student, please use your official student University of Melbourne email address only in this section.

Section B - Academic entry requirements

There are a number of different research options at the MGSE. Please check you meet the academic entry requirements for the research program you are interested in before commencing your application:
  • PhD – Education
  • Doctor of Education
  • Master of Education
  • Master of Philosophy – Education

What if I don’t meet the entry requirements?

If you don’t meet the entry requirements for your preferred research program, you will need to provide us with information so we can make a case on your behalf. Please add a section in your CV entitled ‘Information for making a case’ and include:

PhD – Education

  • Capacity to reach an H2A average and/or
  • Research equivalence – Normally, evidence of such equivalence would take the form of a research publication record and/or additional significant research experience that would be equivalent to a fourth year. In the case of a three year honours degree from the UK, the sponsoring department may also provide evidence about the quality of the institution and the quality of the degree compared with a four year honours degree; and/or
  • Currency of your knowledge of the discipline – demonstrate with examples how you have kept up with current knowledge (reading, conferences, through professional experience).

Doctor of Education

Please note: five years professional experience is necessary to be considered for the DEd. If you do not meet this requirement, a case cannot be made for you to be considered for this program.

Evidence of capacity to undertake research (for example a research Masters thesis, sustained policy document/s, conference presentations, articles in professional journals, etc.).

Master of Education

Evidence of equivalent professional experience that demonstrates teaching skill and capacity and grounding in the Education discipline.

Master of Philosophy - Education

  • In exceptional circumstances, the Research Higher Degrees (RHD) Committee may consider an applicant who does not have qualifications equivalent to a four year honours degree if the applicant's experience in research and the results of that experience are so outstanding that the applicant is likely to be able to pursue the degree successfully. In such cases, the department may present an evidence-based case for admittance to the Chair, RHD Committee.
  • Normally, the evidence of research equivalence would take the form of a research publication record and/or additional significant research experience that would be equivalent to a fourth year. In the case of a three year honours degree from the UK, the sponsoring department may also provide evidence about the quality of the institution and the quality of the degree, compared with a four-year honours degree.

Section B - Research area supervisor

Please do not make contact with a potential supervisor. You can browse potential supervisors and topics using Find an Expert. If you identify a preferred MGSE supervisor, please list them in this section and when we assess your application we will take your suggestion into account.

Section B – Residency requirements

Please ensure you read and understand the residency requirements for the research program you are interested in:

  • PhD – Education
  • Doctor of Education
  • Master of Education
  • Master of Philosophy – Education

A case may possibly be made to waive residency requirements if you are applying for a PhD or MPhil. Please read “Satisfying the residency requirement by other means” in the relevant linked pages above. Please note: A case cannot be made if you are applying for a DEd or MEd because of the substantial coursework components in this program.

Section C – Academic qualifications

Program length refers to the official full-time duration of the program not the time that you might take to complete the program. For example, the program length of the Master of Education at the University of Melbourne is 1.5 years even though, a student make take 3 years to complete this program part-time.

Section D – Research and/or relevant professional experience

For your professional experience to be considered, you must submit proof of employment for every relevant professional experience listed on your CV. This is essential for scholarship and DEd applicants as professional experience without proof of employment will not be considered. Proof of employment must specify employment duration, time fraction and position description. This information can be provided using the Employer Referee Report Form.

Section E – English language requirements

You must meet The Melbourne Graduate School of Education English language requirements to receive an unconditional offer. No cases can be made to waive this requirement.

Section F – Scholarships

There are a number of scholarships available to graduate research students. Please note the scholarship closing dates.

If you are applying for a scholarship, you must submit proof of employment for every relevant professional experience listed in your CV. This may include an Employer Referee Report Form.

Section G – Academic referee reports

Please ensure you use the Academic Referee Report form on the Future Students website when providing academic references.

If it is more than five years since you have been enrolled in a tertiary course, you may provide the names of two employer referees in lieu of academic referees, using the Employer Referee Report Form on the Future Students website. However, if you are applying for a scholarship or the DEd program, you will need to submit proof of employment as part of your application even if you also have acquired two academic referees.

Additional elements to consider

CV

The CV is a vital part of your application and a sample of the appropriate format is provided on page 15 of the RHD application form, Note that this is an example only and not a template for a CV.

Please note that Sections D and F specify essential requirements that should be listed in your CV and in your Research Proposal.

Extended research proposal

In addition to the 100 word research project summary, please provide a 1000 word extended research proposal (attached to your CV) that:
  1. addresses the research interest you wish to investigate
  2. provides the rationale and aims of your study (e.g. the problem you want to investigate or questions you want to ask, and why these are important)
  3. gives one or two examples of the literature you feel supports your topic
  4. details previous research experience (or study around conducting research) that you have
  5. suggests up to four six-digit Field of Research (FoR) codes that describe your intended area of research.

Checklist of Supporting Documentation

You will see a checklist on page 14 of the RHD application form. We recommend you use our MGSE specific checklist which provides more clarity on what supporting documents you need to supply with your application. We highly recommend you work through this checklist before submitting your application.

Apply now

You can access the RHD Application form on the Future Students website and send (by mail or person) to:

Graduate Research Admissions
Office of Admissions Information Centre
Gate 3, Swanston St
University of Melbourne VIC 3010
(Opening hours: Monday to Friday, 8.00am – 6.00pm)

Please note, applications take between 4 to 8 weeks following the application deadline to be processed.

Scholarships & Financial Support

The University of Melbourne offers one of the most comprehensive and generous scholarship programs in Australia, which provide access to a world class university for the best Australian and international students.

A range of financial support initiatives is also available to help pay for graduate study and alleviate the financial pressures while studying.

Finance and Loans

Australian Government loan schemes

FEE-HELP and HECS-HELP are available to help you defer all or part of your tuition fees, depending on the type of offer you receive. With both schemes, you do not need to repay the loan until your income reaches a minimum repayment threshold.

  • If you receive an offer for an Australian fee-paying place, you may be eligible for the FEE-HELP loan program.
  • If you receive an offer for a Commonwealth Supported Place, you may be eligible for the HECS-HELP loan program.
  • For more information on these loan schemes, visit www.studyassist.gov.au

University of Melbourne Financial Aid Services

Financial Aid provides advice and information on the cost of living in Melbourne, student loans and grants, government student assistance, budgeting, tax, and on a range of other matters related to student finances.

Graduate Access Melbourne Program

Graduate Access Melbourne is the University of Melbourneâ€™s special entry and access scheme for graduate study and provides access to applicants whose personal circumstances have had a sustained adverse effect on their academic achievement at undergraduate level or who are members of a specified group known to be underrepresented in higher education.

Graduate Access Melbourne applications are now considered for all MGSE courses with the exception of:
  • Master of Tertiary Management
  • Postgraduate Certificate Mathematics Teaching (Years 7-10)
  • Postgraduate Certificate Content and Language Integrated Learning
  • Postgraduate Certificate Educational Studies (International Baccalaureate) Primary Years
  • Graduate Certificate University Teaching
  • Graduate Certificate University Management
  • Graduate Certificate Quality Assurance

Find out more on the Graduate Access Melbourne website.

Master of Teaching and MGSE Research scholarships

Jennifer Duggan Leaper Scholarship

The Jennifer Duggan Leaper Scholarship is a biennial scholarship offered to a University of Melbourne Bachelor of Arts graduate studying the Master of Teaching who has shown academic excellence in their undergraduate degree but faces financial hardship in completing their Masters. The recipient will receive approx. $10,000 pa towards the cost of tuition and other expenses for the two year duration of their studies.

The Olive Battersby Scholarship

The Olive Battersby Scholarship is an annual scholarship offered to a student enrolled or to be enrolled in the Master of Teaching who faces financial hardship in completing their Masters. The recipient will receive approx. $10,000 towards the cost of tuition and other expenses.

International Postgraduate Coursework Awards (IPCA)

Available to international Master of Teaching students. Based on academic merit and determined by individual faculties. Selection is highly competitive.

William and Kate Herschell Scholarship

This scholarship is awarded annually to an APA or MRS recipient undertaking studies in the Melbourne Graduate School of Education. It is funded through the William and Kate Herschell Scholarship Fund. Interested applicants should direct enquiries to education-herschell@unimelb.edu.au. Benefits $10,000 per annum.

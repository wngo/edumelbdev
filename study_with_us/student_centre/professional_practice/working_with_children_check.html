Working with Children Check

Please note: This information is for Melbourne Graduate School of Education students ONLY.

What is a Working with Children Check?

In 2006, the Victorian Government introduced a new checking system to help protect children under 18 years of age from physical or sexual harm. The Working with Children Check (WWC Check) creates a mandatory minimum checking standard across Victoria.

Find out more about the WWC check at the Department of Justice working with children check website.

If you have current registration with the Victorian Institute of Teaching you are not required to obtain a Working With Children Card but you must provide a copy of this card to the Education Student Centre.

What happens if I don’t have a WWC Check?

If we do not receive a copy of your WWC card (or evidence of your lodgment – the application receipt) you will be unable to commence any of the practicum requirements of your course.

What happens if I already have a WWC card?

If you already have a Volunteer or Employee WWCC you will need to update your details immediately on the working with children website to include the Melbourne Graduate School of Education (MGSE). Alternatively you can add these details over the phone by calling the WWCC Unit on 1300 652 879.

How do I apply for a WWC Card?

  1. Visit the Working With Children Check application page and read the information provided. There are also instructions here for interstate applicants.  You must apply for a Victorian WWCC even if you already have one in another Australian state.
  2. Apply Online. Don’t forget to apply as a volunteer to avoid any fees. 
  3. When you come to the section, "Organisation Details" you need to proved the following information:

Name : MGSE
Address: 234 Queensberry Street
Suburb: Melbourne University
State : Vic
Postcode : 3010
Phone : 8344 0355
Code Number: 52 Volunteer

Print off your ‘Working With Children Check – Volunteer Application Summary’ and ‘Your Volunteer Application Summary Receipt’ and take to any Australia Post Office with 100 points of identification and a passport sized photo of yourself. View find proof of identification requirements. 

Once submitted Australia Post will receipt ‘Your Volunteer Application Summary Report’. Please bring or mail a copy of this receipt to the Education Student Centre, 234 Queensberry Street, University of Melbourne. 

Additional Information

Working With Children Checks became mandatory for kindergartens and childcare centres from June 2008. Please note that some kindergartens and childcare centres may have a local policy and ask students for a current Criminal Records Check. These forms are available from Australia Post Offices or the Victoria Police Website.

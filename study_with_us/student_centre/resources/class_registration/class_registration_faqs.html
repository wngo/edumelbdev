Class Registration FAQs

When do I register for classes?

The registration period for subjects usually opens four weeks prior to the commencement of classes and closes at the conclusion of the second (or first, for MTeach) week of classes. Students are notified of registration periods through the Portal, and MTeach candidates of place release schedules via email and here online.

For which subjects do I need to register into classes?

You will need to register into all scheduled activities/classes (each different workshop, lecture, tutorial, seminar etc) for each subject you are enrolled in for the semester. MTeach candidates do not need to register for Professional Practice subjects, which comprise school placement and have no scheduled classes.

You can refer to the online Handbook (or, for MTeach candidates, the current registration schedule) for details.

How can I generate a clash-free timetable?

To register for classes for your enrolled subjects:
  1. Go to your Student Portal at https://my.unimelb.edu.au/studentportal and log in.
  2. Click on the ‘My timetable’ button in the Student Admin portlet on the home page. This will take you to the student timetable page.
  3. Set the Study Period drop menu to ‘all’ and refresh.

  4. Click the ‘Register’ button next to each enrolled subject for the semester and check the box of your preferred available class for each activity. Remember to save your selected registrations for each subject as you go through (‘Save’ buttons are at the immediate top and bottom of the timetable graphic).

    MEd students, please be aware that some subjects, such as those offered online or research-based, won’t have scheduled classes and therefore do not require class registration.

  5. When you have registered for all activities for all subjects for the semester, you are done!
  6. If you have an unresolvable clash or any queries/difficulties, contact education-teachingservices@unimelb.edu.au

What do I do if all my subjects aren’t showing?

It might be because you are not enrolled in all your subjects. To check this, you can go to the ‘View Study Plan’ link on the home page of your Student Portal – ensure all subjects are marked as ‘enrolled’ and not simply ‘planned’.

If you are enrolled in all your subjects, the most likely reason you’re not able to view all subjects is because the study period drop menu setting needs changing to read ‘All’ and then refreshed.

There are no classes available/I can’t register into classes.

For MTeach candidates, it’s possible that many class release quotas will fill quickly from the release times, so it may be necessary to wait for the next staggered release date (as described on the MTeach registration schedule) before it’s possible to secure a class.

For all other students, if there are no classes available and you are unable to complete your registration, please contact education-teachingservices@unimelb.edu.au for assistance.

What if I have already been registered for a class?

It is possible that you will find you have been automatically registered for some classes, for which there is only one class option. For instance, if there is one lecture for a subject, we may have bulk registered enrolled students, to save you the trouble. This will happen more regularly towards the end of the registration period.

I can’t get into the class I want.

In most cases, this will require you to register for another class, and possibly to reshuffle your timetable. The only acceptable reasons for priority registration are for ongoing medical, religious or childcare arrangements, for which documented evidence will need to be provided. No consideration will be given to registration requests on grounds of work/sporting/travel or similar personal commitments, as the expectation is that you have availability to attend any classes.

I don’t have any breaks in my schedule.

It may be necessary to timetable some whole days with back-to-back classes. Please note that time is provided for room transitions between classes. Classes will finish five minutes before the scheduled end time, and start five minutes after the scheduled commencement, to allow students time to move between rooms/buildings. There is a 15-20 minute break in the middle of the day, during which those with full days can have a quick lunch.

I have a timetable clash.

For Master of Education students, this will require you to change your enrolment to avoid the clash, as most subjects will only have one time option available. You can view the timetables of subjects before enrolling – to ensure they don’t present a clash – in the Handbook at https://handbook.unimelb.edu.au/view/2013/960ac.

For MTeach candidates, this might mean you have to reshuffle your registrations to fit everything in. Unfortunately, it’s not always possible to secure your first class preferences, or for them to work with the scheduling of other subjects, and sometimes (especially after classes start to fill up) it may mean changing several registrations to find a clash-free option. If you can’t find a solution to your timetable clash, contact us in person or at the address below.

What if I’ve tried everything and still can’t successfully register
for classes?

Contact our class registration team. You can email us at education-teachingservices@unimelb.edu.au or visit us through the Education Student Centre.

Class Registration

Overview

The Education Teaching Services Team works to provide an equitable and consistent registration service for students, in accordance with MGSE policy.

While students are given the opportunity to register themselves into classes, with many subjects offering a choice of class times, it is expected that students will have availability to attend any of the classes scheduled for subjects in which they are enrolled.

...read more

We do provide a courtesy priority registration service to eligible students who apply by the set deadline (read below for further details), but when a class is filled, MGSE policy is that no further registrations into this class can be made.

Because our timetables are often complex in their working, given the unique nature of many of our programs, we do not have the flexibility to priority register all students around their personal and work commitments and, because we can’t do this for all students, we are not able to do this for any student. This is a policy point in the direct interests of student equity.

Main Policy Points:

As a quick guide, please note the following main policy points, which are explained in more detail in following sections:

  1. All students must generate their own clash-free, complete timetables by the commencement of classes.
  2. Students must check their University email regularly, as this is the main form of course- and subject- specific registration communication. Essential news and updates will be reported through email.
  3. It is expected that all students will have availability to attend any classes between 8am – 6pm.
  4. Priority registration is offered to students with documented religious/medical/childcare arrangements in place, and actioned where possible.
  5. Work and similar personal commitments are NOT grounds for priority registration – please do not contact Education Teaching Services with these requests.
  6. Students are NOT to approach teaching staff members with registration requests.
  7. Students requiring help with registration can contact ETS at education-teachingservices@unimelb.edu.au .

Registration Process

Once enrolment into subjects has been finalised, students will be required to register for classes within subjects, through a registration period opening approximately four weeks prior to the commencement of classes each semester.

Students register for classes and create their timetables through the Student Portal, and are encouraged to check email regularly for news specific to the programs and subjects in which they are enrolled.

Registration for Master of Teaching

Registration for all streams of the Master of Teaching is usually staggered, with releases of places scheduled over set dates (eg. 50% of places on the first release date, 30% on the second date and 20% on the final date, or similar).

The staggered release is designed to provide a more equitable registration experience for Teacher Candidates (who may not be able to access the system on a given release date) and to promote consistency in class sizes. The staggering schedule, including dates and quotas, is made available to MTeach candidates prior to and at the commencement of each registration period.

Registration for MTeach candidates should be finalised by the Friday before the commencement of classes (those not registered by this time will be auto-allocated), but will officially close in the first week of semester. It is the responsibility of teaching candidates to have created a complete and clash-free timetable by this time.

Priority Registration

Priority registration will be offered to those students with documented medical, religious or child-care arrangements in place. While every effort will be made to accommodate these special requests, please be aware that it may not always be possible to secure all preferred classes. Applications for priority registration are usually invited up to a month before the opening of the registration period, and strongly recommended before the final release of places.

Eligible students with priority registration requests can contact the Education Teaching Servies team, with documented evidence, at education-teachingservices@unimelb.edu.au, or in person/post through the Education Student Centre once the invitation has been made. Relevant evidence might include a letter from a school/childcare centre/health professional, or a birth certificate identifying the applicant to be the parent of a school-aged child. The earlier priority requests are made, the more chance we have of accommodating them, so we urge applicants to contact us as early in the schedule as possible.

Please be advised that under no circumstances will there be priority registration for work commitments of any kind. The expectation of students, in accepting admission to our programs, is that they will have availability to attend any of the scheduled classes between 8am and 6pm.

Registration for Professional Development Programs

Most subjects for our professional development programs, such as the Master of Education, will only have one scheduled class time. Registration into these classes is still a requirement, but students enrolled in these subjects may find they have been automatically bulk registered by our team.

While registration into all classes is a requirement, we urge students studying in intensively-run subjects to refer to the online Handbook, rather than the timetable, for details of class dates, times and venues. It is the responsibility of students to ensure they have a clash-free timetable.

Registration for Breadth Subjects

Students registering for MGSE breadth subjects can do so as any other subject. Please note that MGSE policy also stipulates that no student can be registered into a full class. Students faced with a clash involving an Education breadth subject will need to reshuffle their other registrations to accommodate available classes, or consider another breadth option.

As for all other MGSE subjects, no priority registration can be offered to accommodate work commitments of any kind – please do NOT contact Education Teaching Services with these requests.

Registration Support

A list of some important notes and FAQs, specific to the MTeach registration process, is available here, which we strongly recommend MTeach candidates review before commencing the registration process.

Students are also encouraged to continue checking University email accounts regularly over registration periods, as important updates specific to registration may be sent during this time.

Students still experiencing difficulty in successfully registering for classes or creating a complete, clash-free timetable, can email our team at education-teachingservices@unimelb.edu.au, or visit us through the Education Student Centre.

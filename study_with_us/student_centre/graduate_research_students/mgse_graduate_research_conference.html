MGSE Graduate Research Conference

In 2013 the MGSE is running two annual MGSE Graduate Research Conferences. The first is to be held on Saturday 22 June and the second on Saturday 23 November.

These are free events, open to all graduate research and coursework students in the MGSE. We welcome those who are writing a thesis, those who are interested in writing one, and those who would like to find out more about other students’ research topics. This is an opportunity to hear about and share research experiences across disciplines and themes, in a supportive environment. You can benefit in one or more of the following ways:

  • For all participants, by hearing and discussing what other students are doing – how they are going about it methodologically, across a diverse School, covering early childhood to adult and vocational education –will broaden and deepen your knowledge.
  • For participants presenting your research – by preparing an abstract, and a 20-minute presentation based on this, you focus your work-in-progress, or work-near-completion, and enhance your ‘upfront’ skills.

There will be an opportunity for students in the writing-up stage of their thesis to gain a Manuscript Preparation Support place.

Would you like to be involved in the organisation of the Conference?

This would involve being part of the Conference Committee and working with academic members of the Committee on the following tasks: deciding on the theme for the Conference; assessing the abstracts that are submitted; and grouping the accepted abstracts together for the program. You would attend one or two meetings and offer some time in the week prior to, and on the day of, the Conference, to ensure the smooth running of the event.

If you would like to find out more about the Conference or how to become involved in its organisation contact mgse-research@unimelb.edu.au
